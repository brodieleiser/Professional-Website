Engineering Journey

Bridging Theoretical Physics with Practical Design

As a University of Georgia Mechanical Engineering student, my focus is centered on the critical intersection of computational fluid dynamics (CFD) and structural optimization. I am driven by the challenge of translating complex theoretical physics into high-precision, tangible mechanical designs that solve real-world problems.

My hands-on experience is primarily forged as an active contributor to UGA Motorsports. In this high-stakes environment, I specialize in aerodynamic profiling and ensuring vehicle structural integrity. Leveraging powerful engineering tools like SolidWorks for 3D modeling/FEA and MATLAB for complex simulations, I work with my team to develop and implement next-generation engineering solutions that push the boundaries of performance and safety.

This synthesis of rigorous academic knowledge and competitive, practical application defines my engineering philosophy: innovate with purpose, design for performance, and build for reliability.

Technical Showcase

Selected CAD Designs & Engineering Projects

SolidWorks & FEA

High-Pressure Radial Pump

This project involved a complete 3D assembly and subsequent stress analysis of a radial piston pump designed for high-pressure industrial hydraulic systems. The design process emphasized material selection for durability under extreme pressures and finite element analysis (FEA) to identify and mitigate potential failure points in the casing and piston assemblies. The final model was optimized for manufacturability and serviceability.

Thermal Dynamics & CFD

Cross-Flow Heat Exchanger Prototype

Focused on the urgent need for efficient EV battery cooling, this project included the computational analysis, simulation, and physical prototyping of a novel cross-flow heat exchanger. Using CFD software, various fin geometries and flow path configurations were simulated to maximize the heat transfer coefficient. The final prototype, optimized for compact automotive applications, demonstrated a significant improvement in thermal dissipation over conventional models.

Robotics & Mechatronics

Autonomous Sorting Arm (4-DOF)

This was a comprehensive mechanical design of a 4-degree-of-freedom (DOF) robotic arm for automated sorting tasks. The project featured custom-designed, 3D-printed structural components optimized for a high strength-to-weight ratio. Integration of servo motors with control logic, developed in a Python environment, allowed for precise and repeatable manipulation of objects, demonstrating a full-stack mechatronic system design.
